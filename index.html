Chicago Space Apps 2017

Project Name: Silver Lining

Silver Lining is an online visualization that allows viewers to overlay a world map with different layers of data, such as the amount of various aerosols, the intensity of weather patterns, and the concentration of greenhouse gases. Through viewing these various layers and the extent to which they overlap, the viewer can come to a better understanding of the trends in aerosol distribution and how these coincide with weather patterns and extreme weather events. The project also includes a back-end statistical framework that will allow the viewer to select an area on the map, and then carry out spatial regression analysis on the data available for that area. The analysis will be done on the back-end, rather than the viewer having to carry out the technical work on their own. The aim of this is to allow laymen to better understand the strength of the correlations and relationships between aerosols and weather.
